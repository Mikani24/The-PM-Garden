Implementation

Executing the plan — turning strategy into action

Implementation is where plans meet reality. This is the doing phase: coordinating tasks, managing people, tracking progress, solving problems, and keeping everything moving towards your objectives.
Key concept
Rhythm and routine drive execution

Consistent check-in points keep the team aligned and surface issues before they become crises. Even 10-minute daily standups can be transformative for small teams.

1

Day-to-day execution

Each person shares: what they did yesterday, what they're doing today, and any blockers. Weekly reviews track whether you're on course. Consistency is what matters.

Project Execution and Monitoring
!
Pro tip
Document decisions as you go. A simple decision log saves enormous time later (Wysocki, 2019).
2

Tracking progress

Traffic light system

Green: On track
Amber: At risk
Red: Off track — escalate

Budget check

If you're 50% through the timeline but spent 70% of budget, something needs to change. Excel works perfectly for this.

3

Handling scope creep

Scope creep happens through seemingly reasonable requests. The solution: manage changes deliberately.

!
Watch out
Any change request gets written down, assessed for impact on time/cost/resources, and formally approved or rejected (PMI, 2021).
✓
Best practice
A one-page change request form and a quick conversation with your sponsor is often all you need.
4

Communication

Your team wants detail: tasks, deadlines, dependencies.
Your sponsor wants the headline: on track? Decisions needed?
Stakeholders want reassurance: key milestones and what's next.
Create a comms plan : who, what, how often, which channel.
