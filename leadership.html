Leadership

Guiding people and decisions through complexity and change

Leadership in project management extends beyond holding a title. It's about influence, vision, and guiding a team through uncertainty. For small businesses and charities, effective leadership often emerges from passion and commitment rather than formal hierarchies.
Key concept
Situational leadership

Effective leaders adapt: directive with newcomers, coaching as competence grows, supporting experienced members, delegating to experts (Hersey & Blanchard, 1969).

1

Leadership styles

Goleman (2004) identified six: Visionary, Coaching, Affiliative, Democratic, Pacesetting, Commanding. The best leaders flex between them.

What Makes a Great Leader? — TED-Ed
When to direct

New teams, unclear tasks, time-critical situations. People need clarity.

When to empower

Experienced teams, creative work. People need autonomy and trust.

2

Servant leadership

Greenleaf (2002) turned the hierarchy upside down: the leader serves the team. Removing obstacles, providing resources, creating conditions for best work.

✓
Powerful for
Charities and volunteer organisations where people are motivated by purpose, not salary.
3

Emotional intelligence

EI: recognising and managing emotions, is consistently linked to effective leadership. Five components: self-awareness, self-regulation, motivation, empathy, social skills.

!
Pro tip
EI can be developed. Reflective journaling, seeking feedback, and deliberately pausing before reacting all build emotional intelligence.
4

Leading through change

Communicate relentlessly. People need the message multiple times through multiple channels.
Connect to purpose. Not "new system" but "easier to serve clients."
Celebrate early wins. Visible progress builds momentum (Kotter, 2012).

"The task of leadership is not to put greatness into people, but to elicit it, for the greatness is there already."

— John Buchan
