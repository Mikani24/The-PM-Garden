Strategy

Setting direction and defining goals

Strategy is where every successful project begins. Before you allocate a single resource or assign a task, you need to know where you're heading and why. For small businesses and charities in Ireland, strategic thinking doesn't require expensive consultants: it requires clarity, focus, and honest assessment.
Key concept
Strategy is your project's compass

It answers: Why are we doing this? What will success look like? How does this fit our bigger picture? When scope creep threatens or stakeholders disagree: your strategy is the reference point.

1

What is project strategy?

Project strategy bridges your organisation's mission and specific projects. For a small business, it connects a product launch to your growth plan. For a charity, it ensures a programme aligns with your founding mission and beneficiary needs.

A well-defined strategy acts as a filter for every decision: helping you say "no" to distractions and "yes" to opportunities that advance your goals (Kerzner, 2022).

"Strategy without tactics is the slowest route to victory. Tactics without strategy is the noise before defeat."

— Sun Tzu, The Art of War
2

Strategic analysis tools

Several practical tools can help and none require expensive software.

SWOT Analysis

Examines Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, Threats. The key is honesty. A SWOT is only useful if it reflects reality, not aspiration.

PESTLE Analysis

Scans Political, Economic, Social, Technological, Legal, Environmental factors. Particularly relevant for Irish organisations given EU regulatory changes.

3

Aligning with the UN SDGs

The 17 Sustainable Development Goals provide a universal framework for positive impact. Aligning your strategy with relevant SDGs unlocks funding, improves reputation, and ensures your work contributes to broader progress.

The ECCSR Framework
Ethical, Collaborative, Creative, Sustainable, Results-focused

Developed by Houghton (2022), ECCSR ensures your strategy serves not just project goals but broader societal values.

For charities

A food security charity aligns with SDG 2 (Zero Hunger) and SDG 12 (Responsible Consumption).

For businesses

A tech company building accessibility tools contributes to SDG 10 (Reduced Inequalities) and SDG 9 (Innovation).

4

Practical tips

!
Pro tip
A clear one-page strategic brief answering why, what, for whom, by when, and how you'll measure success is far more valuable than a 50-page document nobody reads.
Involve your team early. Strategy developed in isolation rarely survives contact with reality.
Review and adapt regularly. The Irish business environment changes rapidly. Your strategy should be a living document.
Connect to your mission. Every choice should trace back to your organisation's core purpose.
Measure what matters. Define 3-5 key success metrics upfront.
