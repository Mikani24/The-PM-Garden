Teams

Building, motivating, and managing effective project teams

No project succeeds without the right people working well together. Understanding group dynamics, building trust, resolving conflict, and harnessing diverse skills are core project management capabilities.
Key concept
Tuckman's stages of team development

Teams move through: Forming (cautious), Storming (conflicts emerge), Norming (working norms), Performing (highly effective). Small teams move faster but the stages still happen (Tuckman, 1965).

1

Understanding team dynamics

In Forming, provide clear direction. During Storming, facilitate conflict resolution. At Norming, encourage. At Performing, trust and delegate.

Tuckman's Stages of Group Development — Sprouts
Did you know?

Don't be alarmed by Storming, it's necessary. Teams that skip it have unresolved tensions that surface later at the worst time.

2

Belbin team roles

Belbin (2010) identified nine roles. Effective teams have a mix: no single person fills all roles.

Thinking roles

Plant: Creative ideas
Monitor Evaluator: Analysis
Specialist: Expert knowledge

Action roles

Shaper: Driving progress
Implementer: Ideas into action
Completer Finisher: Quality

!
Pro tip
In small teams, each person naturally covers 2-3 roles. Simply being aware of gaps helps you compensate.
3

Virtual and hybrid teams

Over-communicate. Informal coffee-machine conversations don't happen virtually.
Use collaborative tools. Trello, Asana, Monday.com replace the whiteboard.
Watch for isolation. Regular one-to-ones maintain cohesion.
4

Resolving conflict

Healthy disagreement leads to better decisions. The key: distinguish task conflict (about work) from relationship conflict (personal friction).

✓
Best practice
Address conflict promptly. Listen to all perspectives, focus on interests not positions, and look for solutions addressing underlying needs (Lencioni, 2002).
