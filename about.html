Why the PM Garden exists and how it was made

The PM Garden

The PM Garden is an online project management guide designed specifically for small businesses and charities working in Ireland. It was created by Anikó Mikolasek as the individual assignment for BMGT45510 (Company Project) at UCD Michael Smurfit Graduate Business School.

Special thanks to Joe Houghton for his guidance, the ECCSR framework, and his encouragement to explore AI tools as part of the learning journey.

The garden metaphor reflects the core belief of this guide: that good project management helps organisations grow. Like tending a garden, it requires patience, planning, the right tools, and constant attention. Each topic is a petal on the flower: interconnected and essential for healthy growth.

Purpose & Audience

This guide is aimed at:

  • Small business owners in Ireland who need practical, jargon-free PM guidance
  • Charity and non-profit staff managing funded projects for the first time
  • Volunteer coordinators looking for structured approaches to organising work
  • Anyone who wants to understand PM fundamentals without wading through dense textbooks

The content bridges academic best practice and real-world application, drawing on both peer-reviewed literature and practical business resources.

AI Disclosure

Use of AI in this assignment

In accordance with the module's AI usage policy, the following disclosure outlines how artificial intelligence tools were used in the preparation of this website:

  • Tool used: Claude (Anthropic) — claude.ai
  • Website design and code: Claude was used to assist with generating the HTML, CSS, and JavaScript code for the website layout, interactive flower navigation menu, and page templates. All design decisions (garden theme, colour choices, structure) were directed by the student.
  • Content drafting: Claude was used to help draft initial content for topic pages. All content was reviewed, edited, and supplemented with the student's own analysis, insights, and references to ensure originality and academic rigour.
  • AI Chatbot: The site includes an AI-powered chatbot using the Claude API to help visitors explore PM concepts interactively.
  • Research assistance: Claude was used to help identify and structure relevant academic references. All references were verified independently for accuracy.
