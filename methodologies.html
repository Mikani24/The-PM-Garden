Methodologies

Agile, Waterfall, PRINCE2 — choosing the right approach

There is no single 'right' way to manage a project. The best methodology depends on your context: the nature of the work, the certainty of requirements, your team's experience, and stakeholder expectations.
1

Waterfall — traditional

Linear: define, design, build, test, deliver. Works when requirements are clear, the deliverable is well understood, and documentation is required (Wysocki, 2019).
What is Agile?
2

Agile — embracing flexibility

Iterative delivery in short sprints, getting feedback and adapting. Scrum uses 2-4 week sprints with standups, reviews, and retrospectives.

✓
Great for
Uncertain requirements, feedback-driven work, teams comfortable with ambiguity. Liberating for charities exploring new territory (Schwaber & Sutherland, 2020).
3

PRINCE2 — structured governance

Comprehensive framework widely used in UK/Ireland. Clear roles, defined stages, management by exception.

!
Pro tip
PRINCE2 is designed to be tailored. Adopt its principles (business justification, learn from experience, defined roles) without the full apparatus.
4

Choosing wisely

Ask yourself

Requirements clear? → Waterfall
Lots of feedback needed? → Agile
Funder expects governance? → PRINCE2
Answer is "it depends"? → Hybrid

Hybrid is fine

Waterfall planning + Agile sprints, PRINCE2 governance + daily standups. The methodology serves the project, not the other way around.

"The ECCSR framework reminds us to keep focus on sustainable, ethical results through collaboration — whichever methodology you choose."

— Houghton (2022)
