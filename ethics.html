Ethics

Responsible, fair, and transparent project practices

Ethics in project management means doing the right thing, even when it's difficult. Even when nobody is watching. It covers honesty in reporting, fairness in procurement, respect for people, and transparency in decision-making.
Key concept
Four core values

Responsibility (own decisions), Respect (people, resources), Fairness (impartial dealing), Honesty (truthful communication) — the PMI Code of Ethics (2006).

1

Why ethics matter

A charity that misuses funds or a business that misleads customers destroys the trust small organisations depend on for survival.
Creating ethical cultures in business

"Ethics is the foundation — the 'E' in ECCSR. Without it, collaboration becomes manipulation, creativity becomes exploitation, and results become meaningless."

— Houghton (2022)
2

Navigating dilemmas

Identify the dilemma — what competing values are at stake?
Consider all perspectives — who is affected?
Test against principles — honest? Fair? Respectful?
Document reasoning — builds ethical culture.
3

Building ethical culture

✓
Lead by example
Be transparent about status. Treat partners fairly. Acknowledge mistakes. Create safety for raising concerns.
4

Ethics and AI

!
Academic integrity
If you use AI tools, declare this openly. Describe what you used and the prompts. The value is combining AI with your own expertise and critical thinking.
